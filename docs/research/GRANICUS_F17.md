# F17 Granicus research note

**Reviewed:** 2026-07-20
**Dataset:** `granicus-core-0.1.0`

## Scope

This first reviewed vertical slice is deliberately narrow: the Kingdom of Macedon, the Achaemenid Empire, Pella, Sestos, Abydos, the Granicus battlefield region, Alexander III, Darius III, the Battle of the Granicus, Alexander's crossing into Asia, and one conservative 334 BCE territory context for each polity.

## Source method

- Arrian, *Anabasis* I.11–16 supplies the principal ordered narrative from the Hellespont crossing through the battle. The record cites chapter locators rather than treating the whole work as undifferentiated evidence: <https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/46976>.
- Diodorus, *Library* XVII.17–21 is retained because its dawn crossing and tactical sequence conflict materially with Arrian: <https://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Roman/Texts/Diodorus_Siculus/17B%2A.html>.
- Plutarch, *Alexander* 16 is a parallel ancient account: <https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/text?doc=Perseus:text:2008.01.0129>.
- Ernst Badian's *Encyclopaedia Iranica* article “Granicus” is the main modern critical synthesis. It identifies the river with the modern Biga/Kocabaş Çay context while warning that detailed reconstruction and numerical totals are unreliable: <https://www.iranicaonline.org/articles/granicus/>.
- The Iranica biographies of Alexander and Darius anchor life and reign chronology: <https://www.iranicaonline.org/articles/alexander-the-great-356-23-bc/> and <https://www.iranicaonline.org/articles/darius-v/>.
- Metropolitan Museum essays provide institutional syntheses for the Macedonian and Achaemenid polity contexts: <https://www.metmuseum.org/essays/the-rise-of-macedonia-and-the-conquests-of-alexander-the-great> and <https://www.metmuseum.org/essays/the-achaemenid-persian-empire-550-330-b-c>.
- Pleiades stable place records provide representative points for Pella (491687), Sestos (501609), and Abydos (501325): <https://pleiades.stoa.org/>. Getty TGN 6002329 provides the representative Granicus river/battlefield-region point: <https://www.getty.edu/vow/TGNFullDisplay?english=Y&find=7009485&nation=&place=&subjectid=6002329>.

## Editorial decisions

- The battle is dated only to 334 BCE. A more exact day is not encoded.
- Darius III is a related ruler and adversary in the wider campaign, but he was not at the Granicus and is not a participant or commander.
- Forces and losses are omitted because the surviving totals conflict and modern critical synthesis does not support a trustworthy reconstruction.
- The event point is marked `disputed`. It represents the Granicus river/battlefield region, not a surveyed battle coordinate.
- The journey geometry is `schematic`: straight segments connect the authored Sestos, Abydos, and Granicus stages and do not claim roads, landing tracks, or intermediate marching positions.
- Territory geometry is intentionally conservative and low confidence. The Macedonian polygon is a generalized core-region footprint. The Achaemenid polygon shows only western-Anatolian campaign context, not the full empire or exact satrapal borders.
- Public `alexanders-world` membership contains reviewed records only. Synthetic compatibility records remain explicitly classified and are retained outside that public collection.
